Multiply by 25

Home

Multiplying by 25 uses the same principle as multiplication by 5 and 50. We relate 25 to a power of 10.

The Trick:

100 = 25 × 4 so do the following
  1. Divide the number by 4 and write down the answer, ignoring the remainder.
  2. Find the last 2 digits from the list below and write them down at the end.

Note that the last 2 digits can be found from the remainder after division.

  • If the remainder is 0 the last 2 digits will be 00.
  • If the remainder is 1 the last 2 digits will be 25.
  • If the remainder is 2 the last 2 digits will be 50.
  • If the remainder is 3 the last 2 digits will be 75.

You can use the normal division algorithm and write down the digits as you find them out to divide by 4. Just be sure to remember the remainder.

Examples:

Find 140 × 25

  1. 140 ÷ 4 = 35 with remainder 0. Write "35".
  2. Since the remainder is 0 write "00" after the "35". The answer is 3,500.

Find 123 × 25

  1. 123 ÷ 4 = 40 with remainder 3. Write "40".
  2. Since the remainder is 3 write the last two digits of "75". The answer is 4,075.
