Since we use a base 10 number system it is actually easier to multiply by 10 than it is to multiply by 5. Multiplying by 10 requires only adding a zero to the end of the number. So if we can relate 5 to 10 then we can incorporate the 10 trick.

The Trick:

10 = 5 × 2 so do the following
  1. Divide the number by 2.
  2. Multiply the result by 10 and write it down.

Note that all odd numbers will end in a 5 and all even numbers will end in a 0.

Examples:

Find 121 × 5

  1. Half of 121 is 60.5.
  2. 60.5 × 10 = 605. The answer is 605.

Find 12,345 × 5

  1. Half of 12,345 is 6,172.5
  2. 10 × 6,172.5 = 61,725. The answer is 61,725.
