Multiplying numbers whose ones add to ten and have the same ten's digits.

Home

If ab and ac are two numbers with a being the tens digits and b, c being ones digits such that c+b=10 then this trick will apply. The value of ab is 10 × a + b and the value of ac is 10 × a + c. When we multiply these together we get
(10 × a + b) × (10 × a + c) = 100 × a2 + 10 ×ac + 10 × ab + bc = 100 × a2 + 10 ×a × (b + c) + bc = 100 × (a2 + a) + bc = 100 ×a×(a+1) + bc.

The Trick:

We identify a, b and c as above and do the following.
  1. Multiply b and c. Write down the result. This is the last TWO digits of the answer. Single digit numbers must have a 0 in front of them!
  2. Multiply a with the next number after a. This is the rest of the answer. Write it down.

Examples:

Find 24 × 26

  1. Here a = 2, b = 4, and c = 6. b × c = 6 × 4 = 24. 24 are the last 2 digits.
  2. a = 2. So we do 2 × 3 = 6. 6 is the hundreds place. The answer is 624.

Find 111 × 119

  1. Here a = 11, b = 1, and c = 9. b × c = 9 so write down "09".
  2. a = 11, so do 11 × 12 = 132. Write down 132. The answer is 13,209.
